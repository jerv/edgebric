# Acme Corp Employee Handbook

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Version:** 3.2

---

## 1. Employment Policies

### 1.1 At-Will Employment
All employment at Acme Corp is "at-will," meaning either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice.

### 1.2 Equal Opportunity
Acme Corp is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other legally protected characteristic.

### 1.3 Anti-Harassment Policy
Acme Corp maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind. Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it immediately to HR or use the anonymous reporting hotline at 1-800-555-0199.

---

## 2. Compensation & Benefits

### 2.1 Pay Schedule
Employees are paid bi-weekly on Fridays. If a payday falls on a company holiday, payment will be issued the preceding business day. Direct deposit is required for all employees.

### 2.2 Health Insurance
Full-time employees are eligible for health insurance beginning on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment.

- **Medical**: Acme covers 80% of premiums for employee-only plans, 70% for family plans
- **Dental**: Acme covers 100% of employee premiums, 50% of dependent premiums
- **Vision**: Acme covers 100% of employee premiums

Open enrollment occurs annually in November. Life changes (marriage, birth, etc.) qualify for mid-year enrollment.

### 2.3 401(k) Retirement Plan
Acme matches 100% of employee contributions up to 4% of salary, and 50% of the next 2% (maximum match of 5%). Vesting schedule: 25% per year, fully vested after 4 years. Employees are eligible to participate after 90 days of employment.

### 2.4 Salary Reviews
Annual salary reviews take place each April. Performance is evaluated against goals set during the prior review cycle. Merit increases typically range from 2-5%, with exceptional performers eligible for up to 8%.

## 3. Time Off & Leave

### 3.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)
PTO accrues based on tenure:
- **0-2 years**: 15 days per year (1.25 days/month)
- **3-5 years**: 20 days per year (1.67 days/month)
- **6-10 years**: 25 days per year (2.08 days/month)
- **10+ years**: 30 days per year (2.5 days/month)

Maximum PTO balance: 1.5x annual accrual. Unused PTO above the cap stops accruing until the balance is reduced. PTO is paid out upon separation at the employee's current rate.

### 3.2 Holidays
Acme observes 11 paid holidays:
1. New Year's Day
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day
3. Presidents' Day
4. Memorial Day
5. Juneteenth
6. Independence Day
7. Labor Day
8. Thanksgiving Day
9. Day After Thanksgiving
10. Christmas Eve
11. Christmas Day

### 3.3 Sick Leave
Employees receive 10 sick days per year, accruing at 0.83 days/month. Sick leave does not carry over and is not paid out upon separation. A doctor's note is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

### 3.4 Parental Leave
- **Birth parents**: 16 weeks paid leave at 100% salary
- **Non-birth parents**: 8 weeks paid leave at 100% salary
- Additional unpaid leave of up to 12 weeks is available under FMLA

### 3.5 Bereavement Leave
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days paid
- Extended family (grandparent, in-law, aunt/uncle): 3 days paid

## 4. Remote Work Policy

### 4.1 Hybrid Model
Acme operates a hybrid work model. Employees are expected to be in-office a minimum of 2 days per week (Tuesday and Thursday are designated anchor days). Fully remote arrangements may be approved by department heads for roles that do not require in-person collaboration.

### 4.2 Home Office Stipend
Remote and hybrid employees receive a one-time $500 home office setup stipend and a $75/month internet and utilities reimbursement.

### 4.3 Equipment
Acme provides a company laptop, monitor, and peripherals. All company equipment must be returned upon separation. Lost or damaged equipment may be deducted from the final paycheck at depreciated value.

## 5. Code of Conduct

### 5.1 Dress Code
Business casual is the standard dress code. On Fridays and during company events, casual dress is permitted. Client-facing meetings require business professional attire.

### 5.2 Confidentiality
Employees must safeguard confidential company information, trade secrets, and client data. Unauthorized disclosure may result in termination and legal action. NDAs must be signed within the first week of employment.

### 5.3 Social Media
Employees may not represent themselves as speaking on behalf of Acme Corp on social media without prior approval from the Communications department. Personal social media activity should not include confidential company information.

### 5.4 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any outside employment, board memberships, or financial interests that may conflict with their duties at Acme. The Ethics Committee reviews disclosures and provides guidance within 10 business days.

---

## 6. Performance Management

### 6.1 Review Cycle
Performance reviews occur semi-annually in April (annual review) and October (mid-year check-in). Reviews include self-assessment, manager assessment, and optional 360-degree feedback from peers.

### 6.2 Performance Improvement Plans
Employees who receive a "Needs Improvement" rating will be placed on a 60-day Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). During this period, the employee will meet weekly with their manager to track progress against specific, measurable goals.

### 6.3 Promotions
Promotion eligibility requires a minimum of 12 months in the current role and a rating of "Meets Expectations" or above. Promotions are reviewed by the department head and HR during the April review cycle.

---

## 7. Separation

### 7.1 Resignation
Employees are asked to provide at least 2 weeks' written notice. Directors and above are asked to provide 4 weeks' notice. An exit interview with HR is scheduled during the final week.

### 7.2 Termination
Involuntary terminations follow a progressive discipline process: verbal warning, written warning, PIP, termination. Exceptions apply for gross misconduct, which may result in immediate termination.

### 7.3 Severance
Employees terminated without cause with 2+ years of tenure are eligible for severance: 2 weeks' pay per year of service, up to a maximum of 26 weeks. Severance is contingent on signing a separation agreement.
